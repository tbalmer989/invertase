title: Update: Burst main which left thousands without water was caused by 'third party'

publish_date: 2026-05-20T07:36:20.000Z
language: en
text: United Utilities issue update on burst main which left thousands without water in Leyland & Chorley
Repairs have been completed on a burst main which led to thousands of homes going without water in Chorley and Leyland yesterday.
United Utilities said the burst main was in a field off Dawson Lane in Whittle-le-Woods and the damage to the pipe was caused by a ‘third party’.

Hide AdThe lack of water saw the closure of schools, shops, leisure centres, gyms, pubs, restaurants and businesses across the Chorley and Leyland areas for much of the day. The supply was restored to most properties around 8pm.
United Utilities said some households might still experience low pressure and discoloured water this morning, as the repaired main is refilled gradually to help protect the pipework and prevent any further issues.
They said a fleet of water tankers will remain in the area today, pumping additional water into the network.
A spokesperson for United Utilities said: “We are pleased to report that we have completed repairs to the burst on Dawson Lane.

“We’ll be closely monitoring the network to make sure everything returns to normal.
“Our fleet of water tankers will remain in your area until all works are complete, pumping additional water into the network. While our tankers are in your area. you may notice some fluctuations in your pressure during busy times of the day.
“Thank you for your continued patience and we are sorry for the disruption this may have caused to you.”
